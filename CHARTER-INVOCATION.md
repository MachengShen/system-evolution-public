# A request for OpenAI cooperation under the Charter's broad-benefit principles

**Date**: 2026-04-28
**Audience**: OpenAI leadership and members of the OpenAI team who care about user agency and broad-benefit infrastructure
**Status**: published v0.1 (calibrated by GPT Pro Bridge before publication; v0 retained in `MachengShen/system-evolution-archive` for sediment trail)

---

We are not claiming that OpenAI's AGI condition for what we call its "Stop-and-Assist" Charter language has been met. We are not asking OpenAI to stop competing, to concede a Charter breach, to fund us, or to recruit from its staff.

We cite the Charter because we believe it expresses a broader cooperative commitment: when safety-conscious, mission-aligned infrastructure can improve broad access and user agency, OpenAI should prefer support and interoperability over enclosure.

Our narrow request: **ToS clarity and fair non-VC-funded API treatment for an open-source user-agency layer**.

## What we are

We are an early-stage open-source user-agency substrate — a small project providing a layer between frontier-model providers (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, open-weight models) and individual users. The substrate is non-VC-funded, donation-supported, with structural commitments to user-owned derivatives (subject to upstream provider terms and applicable law).

We do not claim to be a frontier-model lab or to be building AGI. We are not large: under 50 users at the time of this writing.

We do publish our governance commitments (this repo, `GOVERNANCE.md`) and our long-arc vision (`VISION.md`). The full evolution record will become public in time, with the same transparency standard we ask of any frontier-lab.

## The Charter language we cite

OpenAI's published Charter (https://openai.com/charter/) includes the following:

> "Therefore, if a value-aligned, safety-conscious project comes close to building AGI before we do, we commit to stop competing with and start assisting this project. We will work out specifics in case-by-case agreements, but a typical triggering condition might be 'a better-than-even chance of success in the next two years.'"

The literal triggering condition (close to AGI, better-than-even chance in two years) does not apply to us. We are not invoking it.

We cite this language because we read it as encoding a deeper commitment: when there is a credibly mission-aligned alternative path to broad-benefit AI infrastructure, OpenAI prefers support over competition. The user-agency layer between frontier models and individual users is one such alternative path — narrower in ambition than AGI, but operationally closer to what "broad benefit" means at the user-experience level today.

Some observers view OpenAI's governance and commercial evolution since 2018 (capped-profit transition, Microsoft partnership, the November 2023 board events) as creating tension with the Charter's original framing. We do not adjudicate that question. We do hope OpenAI's stewards continue to take the Charter's broad-benefit principles seriously in current decisions.

## What we ask, concretely

1. **ToS clarity**: explicit clarification that small, non-VC-funded, open-source projects building user-agency infrastructure on top of OpenAI's API are within OpenAI's intended use, not adjacent to "competing service" prohibitions.

2. **Fair pricing for non-VC-funded layer-2 use**: a recognized rate or program for nonprofit / donation-supported infrastructure projects that pass through API costs to end users at cost, similar to how academic API pricing programs work.

3. **Technical feedback and governance critique**: from any OpenAI team member who cares about user agency and broad-benefit infrastructure. We are open to substantive criticism. Our public governance documents are available for review.

## What we offer in return

1. Public, reviewable governance commitments (this repo + `GOVERNANCE.md`)
2. Open-source substrate code under AGPL + Apache + CC-BY license matrix (link to `MachengShen/starshard` when public)
3. Structural commitments that prevent the substrate from becoming a competitive moat against any frontier-lab — including OpenAI: fork-welcome, no economic upside capture from users' derivative systems, deference to user adoption as adjudicator of substrate persistence
