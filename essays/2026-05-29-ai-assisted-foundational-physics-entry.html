Public-safe research brief

AI-assisted foundational physics entry

A conservative first translation layer for asking whether observerhood, agency, information, memory, feedback, and attractors can be expressed in existing physics languages and tested with small models.

Created: 2026-05-29

Status: internal public-safe draft. Not a claim of new physics.

This is not a proposed theory of everything, not a claim that consciousness causes quantum measurement, not a UAP/new-physics claim, and not an announcement of a finished result. The first goal is to learn where the intuitions break under known physics.

One-sentence thesis

We are building an AI-assisted research program that translates intuitions about observerhood, agency, information, memory, feedback, and attractors into existing physics languages, then tests them with small formal models and reproducible public-data analyses.

Working translation

Intuitive term First operational translation
Observer Measurement channel plus state estimator.
Agent Policy mapping observations or estimates to bounded actions.
Information Mutual information, entropy, predictive information, likelihood, or uncertainty.
Memory Explicit state variable carrying past measurement information, with reset or finite capacity when relevant.
Attractor Basin, metastable state, stationary distribution, limit cycle, phase-locked regime, or recovery dynamic.
Control Bounded intervention with explicit cost, delay, noise, and failure cases.

First two models

1. Feedback and memory reset

Question: when does measurement plus feedback improve an objective after the cost of memory reset is included?

Minimal model: finite-state Markov system, noisy measurement, controller memory, feedback rule, reset cost proxy, and passive/random/no-information baselines.

Why it matters: it forces "information helps action" to pay a thermodynamic accounting cost instead of remaining a slogan.

2. Bounded observer-control of a noisy attractor

Question: when does a noisy or delayed observer-controller improve basin stability, phase locking, or prediction/control tradeoff under matched action cost?

Minimal model: stochastic double-well or driven oscillator, noisy observations, bounded feedback, delay/noise sweeps, and matched-cost baselines.

Why it matters: it turns "agency reshapes attractors" into measurable stability, switching, and cost curves.

First public-data discipline

Default: NASA Exoplanet Archive selection-effect analysis.

Question: how does the observing pipeline shape which worlds become visible in a public scientific catalog?

Why this matters: it gives a concrete, low-risk way to study observer-dependent knowledge without jumping to anomalies or speculative claims.

Guardrails

  • Respect Lorentz invariance/covariance where relevant.
  • Respect unitarity for closed quantum dynamics.
  • Respect no-signaling.
  • Account for thermodynamic costs in measurement, memory, feedback, reset, and reservoirs.
  • Check known experimental bounds before any new-physics language.
  • Treat UAP/anomaly material only as future sensor-chain discipline, not evidence in the first phase.
  • Verify citations and equations from primary/review sources before any public use.

What we would ask a physicist

  1. What existing field is the closest serious home for this framing?
  2. Which terms are category mistakes?
  3. Which equations or known results should constrain the first two toy models?
  4. What would immediately kill this as a research direction?
  5. What is the smallest legitimate model or data analysis that would make the question worth continuing?
